# Toyota Production System (TPS) & Gemba Walk

## Core Pillars
- **Jidoka** ("automation with a human touch") - build quality in at the
  source; stop the line the moment a defect is detected rather than passing
  it downstream. In back-office terms: validate data at entry, not after
  three hand-offs.
- **Just-In-Time (JIT)** - produce/process only what's needed, when needed,
  in the amount needed. Avoid batching that creates queues (inventory waste).

## Gemba Walk
"Gemba" means "the real place." A Gemba walk is a structured, first-hand
observation of the actual process as it is performed - not as documented -
by walking the floor (or, for digital processes, shadowing the actual
screens/systems used). The purpose is to see waste, variation, and
workarounds that no SOP captures. This tool's diagnostic mirrors a Gemba
walk: it asks for every real step, system, hand-off, wait, and workaround,
then classifies waste and improvement opportunity per step.

## Kaizen (Continuous Improvement)
Kaizen means small, continuous, incremental improvements driven by the
people who do the work, as opposed to large disruptive transformation
projects. A Kaizen event (or "Kaizen blitz") is a focused 3-5 day workshop
targeting one process to rapidly implement quick wins. Complements
transformational digital initiatives - quick wins should ship in parallel
with longer digital transformation roadmaps, not wait for them.

## Standard Work
Standard Work documents the current best-known way to perform a task -
takt time, work sequence, and standard in-process stock. Without standard
work, improvement has no stable baseline to measure against, and training
new staff introduces new variation. SOP Improvement and Process
Standardization opportunities should always reference Standard Work
principles: one best way, visually documented, kept current.

## 5S Workplace Organization
Sort, Set in order, Shine, Standardize, Sustain. Applies equally to digital
workspaces: sort unused files/tools, organize shared drives and templates,
standardize naming conventions and folder structures, sustain via periodic
audits. A common quick win for administrative processes.

## Visual Management
Making process status, performance, and abnormalities visible at a glance
(Kanban boards, dashboards, color-coded status). Reduces the "waiting"
waste caused by people not knowing what's next or what's blocked.

## Poka-Yoke (Error Proofing)
Designing a process or system so mistakes are physically or logically
impossible, or immediately obvious - e.g. mandatory fields, dropdowns
instead of free text, validation rules, checksum digits. Directly reduces
Defects waste and rework, and is often a low-cost, high-impact quick win
before investing in full automation.

## SMED (Single-Minute Exchange of Die)
Originally for machine changeovers, applied in services as reducing
setup/switching time between tasks or contexts (e.g. system logins,
context-switching between queues) by converting "internal" setup (must be
done while stopped) into "external" setup (can be done in parallel/ahead of
time).
