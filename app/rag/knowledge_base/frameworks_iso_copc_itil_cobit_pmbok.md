# Governance & Delivery Frameworks: ISO, COPC, ITIL, COBIT, PMBOK

## ISO 9001 (Quality Management) / ISO 27001 (Information Security)
ISO 9001 requires documented processes, defined roles, measured
performance, corrective action on nonconformance, and continual
improvement - directly maps to this tool's SOP Improvement and Governance &
Control recommendation categories. ISO 27001 governs information security
controls - relevant whenever a recommended automation/AI solution touches
PII, credentials, or regulated data; flag data handling and access control
requirements alongside any such recommendation.

## COPC (Customer Operations Performance Center) Standard
A performance management standard for customer-contact operations covering
Leadership, Process, People, and Performance. Emphasizes root-cause driven
corrective action (not just monitoring), balanced scorecards (quality +
efficiency + customer experience, not efficiency alone), and defect
prevention over defect detection. Useful benchmark when a process has a
customer-facing SLA - efficiency recommendations must show they don't
degrade the quality/CX side of the scorecard.

## ITIL (IT Service Management)
Framework for IT service delivery: Incident, Problem, Change, and Request
Management. Relevant whenever a process step depends on IT systems/tickets
- Problem Management's root-cause-before-symptom-fix principle mirrors
Lean root cause analysis, and Change Management principles should gate any
automation/AI change to a production process (impact assessment, rollback
plan, approval).

## COBIT (Control Objectives for Information and Related Technology)
Governance framework for IT risk, control, and value delivery, aligning
technology decisions (including new automation/AI investments) to business
goals with defined accountability (RACI) and risk appetite. Use to frame
Governance & Control recommendations for higher-risk automation initiatives.

## PMBOK (Project Management Body of Knowledge)
Structures delivery of the transformation roadmap itself: scope, schedule,
cost, risk, stakeholder, and change management for each initiative. Every
30/60/90-day and strategic recommendation in the roadmap should carry an
implied PMBOK-style owner, timeline, and risk register entry, not just a
description of the improvement.

## SOP (Standard Operating Procedure) Templates
A good SOP includes: Purpose/Scope, Roles & Responsibilities (RACI),
step-by-step procedure with screenshots/system references, inputs/outputs,
exception handling instructions, escalation path, and a revision history/
owner for periodic review. SOP Improvement recommendations should call out
which of these elements is missing or outdated, not just "update the SOP."
